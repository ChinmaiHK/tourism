Talakaveri

Talakaveri is the sacred origin of the Cauvery River, nestled in the Brahmagiri Hills. A small temple marks the spot where the river emerges as a spring, making it a revered pilgrimage site for devotees.

Nearby, Bhagamandala is known for its confluence of three rivers—Cauvery, Kannike, and Sujyoti. The Triveni Sangam is considered highly auspicious, and visitors often take a dip in its holy waters. The Bhagandeshwara Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva, adds to the spiritual aura of the place.

Located about 48 km from Madikeri, Talakaveri and Bhagamandala are accessible via a scenic drive through misty hills and coffee plantations. The best time to visit is during the Tula Sankramana festival in October, when thousands of pilgrims gather to witness the river’s emergence.
